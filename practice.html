Who want's to become Batman

Created by Bob Kane and Bill Finger in 1939, Batman is the iconic alter ego of Bruce Wayne, a billionaire philanthropist based in the crime-ridden Gotham City. Driven by the childhood trauma of witnessing his parents' murder, Wayne trained his body and mind to peak human perfection, vowing to fight crime. Unlike many superheroes, Batman possesses no supernatural powers, relying instead on his genius-level intellect, detective skills, martial arts mastery, and high-tech gadgets. Often called the "Dark Knight," he operates as a brooding, fear-striking vigilante, patrolling the night to protect his city from a notorious "rogues' gallery" of villains, with support from allies like Alfred Pennyworth and Commissioner Gordon


I am Deadpool, Welcome to the party.

Deadpool, alter ego of Wade Wilson, is a wildly popular Marvel Comics antihero created by Fabian Nicieza and Rob Liefeld, first appearing in New Mutants #98 in 1991. Known as the "Merc with a Mouth," he is a disfigured, mentally unstable Canadian mercenary possessing a superhuman regenerative healing factor that allows him to survive almost any injury. Originally a parody of DC’s Deathstroke, Deadpool became iconic for his chaotic fighting style, dark humor, and unique ability to break the fourth wall, frequently acknowledging his own existence as a comic book and film character. Popularized by Ryan Reynolds in the 2016 film, he is often depicted as a morally ambiguous mercenary navigating the Marvel Universe with katanas, guns, and a deep love for chimichangas.

DC VILLENS

JOKER
The Joker is one of the most iconic and dangerous villains in DC Comics and the archenemy of Batman. Known as the “Clown Prince of Crime,” he is a criminal mastermind who uses humor, chaos, and psychological manipulation to spread fear and destruction in Gotham City. Unlike many villains, the Joker has no clear motive other than creating anarchy and proving that society is fragile and corrupt. His appearance includes white skin, green hair, and a permanent red smile, often caused by a mysterious accident involving chemicals (though his origin story changes in different versions). The Joker relies on clever plans, deadly gadgets, toxins like Joker venom, and his unpredictable mind rather than superpowers. What makes the Joker truly terrifying is his insanity, intelligence, and obsession with Batman, whom he sees as his perfect opposite. He represents chaos, madness, and the dark side of human nature.
DARKSIDE
Darkseid is one of the most powerful and feared supervillains in DC Comics. He is the tyrannical ruler of the planet Apokolips and seeks to conquer the universe by eliminating free will and bringing all life under his absolute control.
LEX LUTHOR
Lex Luthor is one of the most intelligent and influential supervillains in DC Comics and the greatest enemy of Superman. Unlike many villains, he has no superpowers; his greatest strengths are his genius-level intellect, scientific expertise, and vast wealth.
BRAINIAC
Brainiac is one of the most powerful and intelligent villains in DC Comics and a major enemy of Superman. He is typically portrayed as a highly advanced alien android or artificial intelligence from the planet Colu, obsessed with knowledge and perfection.
